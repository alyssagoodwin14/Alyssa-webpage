ABOUT THE CAVES

The Ajanta Caves are located in Maharashtra, India. The caves are around 29 rock-cut Buddhist cave monuments which date from the 2nd century BCE to about 480 CE.The caves include paintings and rock cut sculptures described as among the finest surviving examples of ancient Indian art, particularly expressive paintings that present emotion through gesture, pose and form. This is a great place to visit because of its rich history and beautiful art. For more information on the Ajanta Caves, click here.
